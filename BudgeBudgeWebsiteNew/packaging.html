Here, at Budge Budge Refineries LTD., we have our very own Packaging System. We maintain all safety and hygeine precautions so that the consumers get the best products.


Keep reading below to know more about our In-house Packaging System.

Did you know : Here at Budge Budge Refineries LTD., we follow a No-Touch Packaging Policy so as to maintain health and safety standards.

Bottle - Packaging


Our automated bottle packaging process for edible oil starts with a no-touch bottle blowing phase, where preforms are transformed into consistent bottle sizes and shapes using nitrogen. This nitrogen-assisted blowing process ensures the bottles maintain their structural integrity and quality. The bottles are then filled with precise quantities of oil, ensuring hygiene and product integrity, while nitrogen can also be used to displace oxygen in the bottles, preserving the oil's freshness. Automatic sealing mechanisms are employed to achieve secure and leak-proof packaging, eliminating the need for manual intervention. Labeled bottles are efficiently arranged into cartoons using robotic arms or conveyors, further reducing the risk of contamination and maintaining a sterile environment. This fully automated system, with the use of nitrogen, guarantees speed, accuracy, and reliable packaging of bottles into cartons, ready for distribution, with no hand touches throughout the process.














Jar - Packaging


Our jar packaging process for edible oil begins with precise filling, as empty jars are filled with accurate quantities of oil using automated filling machines. The jars are then securely sealed with caps or lids at the capping station to maintain product freshness and prevent leakage. Labeled jars are systematically arranged into cartoons using robotic arms or conveyors, ensuring efficient and consistent packing for distribution and retail.







Pouch - Packaging


In the pouch packaging process for edible oil, pre-formed flexible pouches are filled with precise quantities of oil using automated machines. The pouches are then sealed using heat sealing or zipper sealing technology to preserve product freshness and prevent any leaks. Labeled pouches are efficiently packed into cartons using robotic arms or conveyors, ensuring secure and organized packaging for distribution and retail.
















Tin - Packaging


The tin packaging process for edible oil starts with tin sheet preparation, where sheets are cut and formed into cans. Automated filling equipment precisely dispenses oil into each tin can, minimizing waste. Sealing machines hermetically seal the cans to prevent leakage. Labeled cans are then packed into cartons using robotic arms or conveyors, ensuring efficient and secure packaging for distribution and retail.
